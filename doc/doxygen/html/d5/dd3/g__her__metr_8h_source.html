19 /* Our choice for stroke width, in terms of virtual pixels. This number is
20  magic: just slightly greater than sqrt(2), so that adjacent strokes that
21  are inclined at a 45 degree angle will overlap as they should.
22 
23  (According to ``Calligraphy for Computers'', the Hershey fonts were
24  designed to be drawn by an electron beam the intensity of which fell to
25  50% at a transverse displacement of one unit.) */
26

33 /* According to Allen Hershey, 1 em = 32 virtual pixels for his alphabets
34  of principal size. But taking into account the width of the strokes
35  (nominally 1 virtual pixel), if the characters are thought of as resting
36  on a baseline, the baseline (and the capline, etc.) should be located at
37  half-integer values of the vertical coordinate. That changes things
38  slightly (an em should be 33 virtual pixels, not 32).
39 
40  Incidentally his recommended spacing between lines is 40 pixels (24 for
41  indexical size), i.e., 1.2 em or so (1 em is the minimum possible
42  spacing).
43 */

117 /* The scaling between distances in Hershey units and distances in user
118  coordinates. Idea is that the font size (i.e. the nominal minimum
119  inter-line spacing) corresponds to HERSHEY_LARGE_CAPHEIGHT Hershey units. */
120 
121 #define HERSHEY_X_UNITS_TO_USER_UNITS(size) \
122  ((size)*((gc->ps * gc->cex / 72.27) / (dd->dev)->ipr[0])/(HERSHEY_LARGE_HEIGHT))
123 
124 #define HERSHEY_Y_UNITS_TO_USER_UNITS(size) \
125  ((size)*((gc->ps * gc->cex / 72.27) / (dd->dev)->ipr[1])/(HERSHEY_LARGE_HEIGHT))

137 /* Some miscellaneous information on typesetting mathematics, taken from
138  Allen Hershey's 1969 TR (see g_her_glyph.c):
139 
140  Subscripts and superscripts, in math text, should be in indexical size.
141  The centerline of subscripts/superscripts would be lowered/raised by 10
142  vertical units.
143 
144  [In principal size the centerline is 9.5 units above the baseline; in
145  indexical size the centerline is 6.5 units above the baseline. So when
146  going to subscripts, the baseline should be lowered by 7 units; when
147  going to superscripts, the baseline should be raised by 13 units. This
148  is not actually the scheme we use; see alabel_str.c. -- rsm]
149 
150  In math text the quantity being supplied with a sub/superscript is
151  typically an italic character. The transitions principal->superscript
152  and subscript->principal are accordingly accompanied by 2 add'l units of
153  horizontal space.
154 
155  In math text, conjuctive/predicative signs should be given a extra
156  spacing of 1/2 en (i.e. 1/4 em), i.e. 8 units in principal size, to
157  either side. This is accomplished by glyph 2198 (or 1198, in indexical
158  size).
159 
160  Case fractions (e.g. \frac34) are formed by raising/lowering
161  indexical-size characters by 12 vertical units; in mathematical text,
162  they should be given a small (4-unit) spacing to either side.
163 
164  Simple limits, for sum and integral signs, are in indexical size, and
165  are raised/lowered by 24 units. */
