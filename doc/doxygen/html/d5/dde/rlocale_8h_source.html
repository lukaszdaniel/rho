57 /*
58  * Windows CJK
59  * In Unicode, there is not a rule about character width.
60  * A letter of breadth is used in a CJK (China, Japan, Korea,
61  * Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Singapore) area, and there are a
62  * letter and a standard (character width is not still prescribed)
63  * of a cord in a country.
64  * Letter width is a problem of a font, but it is a rule route
65  * besides a alphanumeric character that use a breadth letter.
66  * It is generally defined as a breadth letter for a font such
67  * as Japanese.
68  * - Win32
69 
70  * Attempted explanation by BDR
71  * The display widths of characters are not prescribed in Unicode.
72  * Double-width characters are used in the CJK area: their width can
73  * be font-specific, with different fonts in use in different parts
74  * of the CJK area. The tables supplied in many OSes and by Markus
75  * Kuhn are not do not take the exact locale into account. The
76  * tables supplied in rlocale_data.h allow different widths for
77  * different parts of the CJK area, and also where needed different
78  * widths on Windows. (The Windows differences are in zh_CN, and
79  * apply to European characters.)
80  *
81  * The differences are mainly (but not exclusively) in the
82  * Unicode 'East Asian Ambiguous' class.
83  *
84  */
